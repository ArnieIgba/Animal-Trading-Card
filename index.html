Species Some of the most well-known species of eagles are the bald eagle, the golden eagle, the harpy eagle, and the Philippine eagle. Each species has its own unique features and adaptations to its habitat. For example, the bald eagle has a white head and tail, and feeds mainly on fish. The golden eagle has a golden-brown plumage and hunts mammals such as rabbits and marmots. The harpy eagle has a crest of feathers on its head and preys on monkeys and sloths in the rainforest. The Philippine eagle has a long, shaggy mane and is one of the largest and rarest eagles in the world.
